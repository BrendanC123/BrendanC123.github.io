Hierarchical State Machine and A* Path Finding

Creating the AI

This AI was created for my AI module in second year. I was provided with the pre existing framework of the cars and graphics system and waypoint manager (there is a waypoint at every road intersection). I needed to create an AI to control both cars.

There are 3 top level states that control the cars behaviour. Each entered by pressing 1, 2, or 3, on the keyboard.

The first state is the steering behaviour state. When put into this state the car can perform one of many steering behaviours depending on the sub state it is put in. The wander state is the default state and can be entered by pressing W. This state causes the red car to wander aimlessly. Pressing S causes the blue car to seek to a random waypoint and when it gets there it seeks to a different random waypoint. Pressing A causes the blue car to arrive at a random waypoint, decelerating until it's there. When in the wander state pressing P causes the blue car to chase the red car. And when in the wander state pressing F causes the blue car to flee the red car, it stays stationary when the red car isnâ€™t within range.

The second state is the pathfinding state. The blue car drives to the passenger icon. It does this by travelling on the road using the A* to find the fastest path. Once the passenger is collected it does the same and paths to the next passenger.

The third state is the decision making state. In this state the AI has to balance fuel and speed boosts to collect passengers as efficiently as possible while not running out of fuel.
